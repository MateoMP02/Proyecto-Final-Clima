Weather Glossary

Temperature

Temperature is the measure of the amount of heat in the air. It is measured in degrees Celsius (°C) or Fahrenheit (°F).

Feels Like

"Feels Like" refers to the perceived temperature by the human body, taking into account factors such as humidity, wind, and solar radiation. Although the actual temperature may be low, the "Feels Like" temperature can feel warmer or colder due to these factors.

Atmospheric Pressure

Atmospheric pressure is the force that air exerts on the Earth's surface due to its weight. It is measured in hectopascals (hPa) and influences weather conditions such as storm formation.

Humidity

Humidity refers to the amount of water vapor in the air. It is measured in percentage (%). High humidity can make the weather feel warmer than the actual temperature.

Cloudiness

Cloudiness refers to the amount of clouds in the sky, measured in percentage. A cloudy sky indicates high cloudiness, while a clear sky has low cloudiness.

Wind Speed

Wind speed measures how fast the air is moving. It is measured in kilometers per hour (km/h) or meters per second (m/s). Wind can influence the "Feels Like" temperature and weather conditions.

Visibility

Visibility refers to the distance at which objects can be seen clearly. Under normal conditions, visibility is high, but it can be reduced in foggy or stormy weather.

Sunrise

Sunrise is the moment when the sun starts to appear on the horizon, marking the beginning of the day.

Sunset

Sunset is the moment when the sun sets below the horizon, marking the end of the day.
